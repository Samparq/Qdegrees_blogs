What exactly is the Healthcare Net Promoter Score (NPS)?

The Net Promoter Score is a way to find out how loyal a customer or patient is by asking them one simple question-

On a scale from 0 to 10, how likely are you to recommend our healthcare services to a friend or family member?

Patients are put into three groups based on their scores, ranging from 0 to 10.

•  Promoters (9–10)- These customers had a good experience, are happy with the product and service, and are very likely to tell others about the provider.


•  Passives (7-8): These patients are somewhat happy with their healthcare provider but not happy enough to support them actively. If a rival gives them a better experience, they might switch.

•  Detractors (0–6)- These customers are unhappy with the services and might tell others not to use them. Bad situations can cause bad reviews online and hurt your image.

•	How to Figure Out the Net Promoter Score?

It's easy to figure out the Net Promoter Score Formula-
Net Promoter Score = (% of Promoters - (% of Detractors))

In this case, the Net Promoter Score would be 70% Promoters, 20% Passives, and 10% Detractors.

70% - 10% = %60

The Net Promoter Score can be anywhere from -100 to 100. A higher score means that patients had a better experience.
