# Turning Customer Feedback Into Action With tNPS

**Transactional Net Promoter Score (tNPS)** is a customer experience metric used to measure how customers feel about a **specific interaction or transaction** with your business.

This interaction could include:

- A product purchase  
- A support call  
- A delivery experience  
- A product onboarding process  

Unlike **Relational NPS**, which measures the overall perception of your brand, **Transactional NPS focuses on a single customer experience**.

With the right **Net Promoter Score tool**, such as **QDegrees**, businesses can collect feedback immediately after important interactions and take action quickly.

This allows companies to improve experiences **in real time**, based on genuine customer feedback.

# The Strategic Value of Transactional Net Promoter Score

One of the biggest advantages of **Transactional NPS** is its ability to help businesses make smarter decisions immediately after customer interactions.

Here are some key benefits:

### Real-Time Customer Insights
Transactional NPS helps businesses understand how customers feel right after a specific interaction, such as a purchase or support call. These insights are fresh, accurate, and more actionable than general feedback.

### Quick Identification of Service Issues
Because the survey focuses on a single event, it becomes easier to identify what went wrong and fix the issue quickly.

### Improved Customer Satisfaction
Acting on transactional feedback shows customers that their opinions matter. This builds trust and strengthens relationships.

### Continuous Improvement
Teams can refine processes after every interaction rather than waiting for long-term reviews.

### Better Business Decisions
Using a reliable **Net Promoter Score tool** allows businesses to make decisions based on real customer data instead of assumptions.

Regular use of transactional NPS keeps your team focused on improving customer experiences at every touchpoint.

# Customizing NPS Surveys for Specific Experiences

Transactional NPS surveys capture customer opinions while the experience is still fresh in their minds.

There are several ways to design these surveys depending on the interaction.

### Post-Purchase Surveys
Sent immediately after a customer completes a purchase. These surveys help determine whether the buying process was smooth and if expectations were met.

### Post-Support Interaction Surveys
Triggered after a customer contacts customer support. These surveys measure how effectively the support team resolved the issue.

### Post-Onboarding Surveys
Sent after a new user completes the onboarding process to evaluate whether the experience was clear and helpful.

### Post-Delivery Surveys
Used after a product or service has been delivered to measure satisfaction with delivery time, packaging, and product condition.

### Post-Feature Usage Surveys
Common in digital products. These surveys are sent after customers use a new or important feature to understand whether it provided value.

Using a **Net Promoter Score tool** helps businesses track these interactions and improve specific customer touchpoints quickly.

# Best Practices to Increase TNPS Survey Response Rates

Getting customers to respond to surveys can be challenging. However, small improvements can significantly increase response rates.

### 1. Keep the Message Short and Clear
Long surveys often get ignored. Focus on one main question and keep the message simple and direct.

### 2. Send the Survey at the Right Time
Timing is critical. Send surveys immediately after the customer interaction so the experience is still fresh in their mind.

### 3. Use a Reliable Platform
Using a survey platform like **QDegrees** simplifies survey creation, distribution, and response tracking.

### 4. Personalize the Survey
Personalized messages improve engagement.

Example:

> *Hi James, how was your recent call with our support team?*

This approach makes customers feel valued rather than treated like a data point.

### 5. Avoid Sending Too Many Surveys
Sending surveys too frequently can lead to survey fatigue. Maintain a balanced frequency to keep response rates high.

# High-Impact Questions for Effective tNPS Surveys

A successful transactional NPS survey starts with a clear and simple question.

### Primary Question

> **How likely are you to recommend [company/product/service] based on your recent experience?**

This question focuses on a single interaction and helps businesses understand how specific moments influence customer perception.

### Follow-Up Question

After the rating, ask:

> **What was the main reason for your score?**

This open-ended question provides deeper insights into customer experiences.

Using a reliable **NPS software or Net Promoter Score tool** helps collect, organize, and analyze these responses efficiently.
