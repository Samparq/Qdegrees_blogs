# What Is an NPS Detractor? Turn Detractors Into Promoters

**Net Promoter Score (NPS)** is a widely used metric that helps businesses measure customer loyalty. It is based on a simple question:

> **“On a scale of 0 to 10, how likely are you to recommend our product or service?”**

Based on their responses, customers are grouped into three categories:

---

## Customer Segments in NPS

### Promoters (9–10)
These are your most satisfied customers. They trust your brand, are loyal, and actively recommend your product or service to others.

### Passives (7–8)
These customers are neutral. They are somewhat satisfied but not enthusiastic. They can easily switch to competitors.

### Detractors (0–6)
Detractors are unhappy customers. They may share negative feedback and can harm your brand’s reputation if their concerns are not addressed.

---

# What Is an NPS Detractor?

An **NPS detractor** is a customer who rates your business between **0 and 6** on an NPS survey.

These customers are:

- Dissatisfied with their experience  
- Less likely to return  
- More likely to share negative feedback  

Many businesses make the mistake of focusing only on promoters. However, **detractors are equally—if not more—important**, because their feedback highlights critical issues that need immediate attention.

Ignoring detractors can lead to:

- Loss of customers  
- Negative word-of-mouth  
- Damage to brand reputation  

---

# How to Identify NPS Detractors

The most effective way to identify detractors is through an **NPS survey**.

### Steps to Identify Detractors

1. Send an NPS survey to your customers  
2. Collect responses  
3. Segment responses based on scores  

- **0–6 → Detractors**
- **7–8 → Passives**
- **9–10 → Promoters**

---

## Other Ways to Identify Detractors

While NPS is the most direct method, you can also use other customer experience metrics:

- **CSAT (Customer Satisfaction Score)** → Measures satisfaction with a specific interaction  
- **CES (Customer Effort Score)** → Measures how easy it was for customers to complete a task  

These metrics help uncover friction points and identify unhappy customers.

---

# How to Respond to NPS Detractors

When dealing with detractors, your approach matters. The goal is not to defend your brand but to **resolve the issue and rebuild trust**.

### Best Practices

- **Avoid being defensive** – Accept responsibility where necessary  
- **Understand the issue** – Analyze the customer’s experience  
- **Create a resolution plan** – Identify actionable steps  
- **Fix the problem** – Implement improvements  
- **Follow up** – Check back with the customer after resolving the issue  

---

# How Detractors Affect Your Business

Detractors can significantly impact your business in multiple ways.

### Negative Reviews

One negative review can outweigh multiple positive ones. Poor feedback can discourage potential customers from choosing your brand.

### Customer Churn

Unhappy customers are more likely to leave and never return.

### Competitor Advantage

Detractors often switch to competitors and may even promote those alternatives if they have a better experience.

---

# How to Turn Detractors Into Promoters

Turning detractors into promoters is possible with the right strategy.

### 1. Encourage Feedback
Create open channels for customers to share their experiences. Listening is the first step toward improvement.

### 2. Close the Feedback Loop
Act quickly on complaints and inform customers once their issues are resolved. This shows that their feedback matters.

### 3. Respond Quickly
Timely responses demonstrate professionalism and reduce the risk of customer churn.

### 4. Show Empathy
Acknowledge the customer’s experience and express genuine concern. Human connection builds trust.

### 5. Be Realistic
Avoid overpromising. Set clear and achievable expectations to rebuild credibility.

---

# Final Thoughts

NPS detractors are not a threat—**unless they are ignored**.

Their feedback provides valuable insights into what is not working in your business. By addressing their concerns and improving their experience, you can:

- Strengthen customer relationships  
- Improve brand reputation  
- Increase customer loyalty  

With the right approach, you can turn dissatisfied customers into loyal advocates and drive long-term business growth.
