# What Is Net Promoter Score (NPS)?

**Net Promoter Score (NPS)** is a customer experience metric used to measure how likely customers are to recommend your business to others.

It helps companies understand:

- Customer loyalty  
- Referral potential  
- Overall brand perception  

### The Standard NPS Survey Question

> **“On a scale of 0 to 10, how likely are you to recommend us to your friends, family, or colleagues?”**

Based on the responses, customers are grouped into three categories.

### Promoters (9–10)

These are your most loyal customers. They love your product or service and actively recommend your brand to others.

### Passives (7–8)

Passives are satisfied but not enthusiastic customers. They are unlikely to promote your brand, but they also don’t actively criticize it.

### Detractors (0–6)

Detractors are unhappy customers who may discourage others from using your product or service.

NPS is one of the most useful **customer experience (CX) metrics** because it provides a clear picture of how customers truly feel about your brand.

# Why Is NPS Important?

Customer emotions drive business growth. When customers love your brand, they return and recommend it to others. If they’re unhappy, they may leave silently—or worse, share negative experiences.

Here’s why NPS is so valuable:

- It provides a **simple way to measure customer loyalty**
- Helps identify your **biggest brand advocates**
- Gives **early warning signals** when customer experience declines
- Helps identify unhappy customers and understand their concerns
- Reveals potential **brand ambassadors**
- Enables benchmarking against competitors
- Helps teams identify issues before they become major problems

If you are not using **Net Promoter Score surveys**, you may be missing critical insights into your customer relationships.

# Example of NPS Calculation

Let’s say your survey results show:

- **70% Promoters**
- **20% Passives**
- **10% Detractors**

Your calculation would be:

**NPS = 70 − 10 = 60**

# 12 Net Promoter Score Facts You Need to Know

Here are some powerful and often overlooked insights about NPS.

## 1. High NPS Companies Grow Faster

Companies with high NPS scores often grow **twice as fast** as their competitors because satisfied customers drive referrals and repeat purchases.

---

## 2. NPS Can Predict Business Growth

NPS is not just about current satisfaction—it also predicts **future business performance**. A higher NPS usually indicates stronger long-term growth.

---

## 3. Detractors Aren’t Always Haters

Sometimes detractors simply had **one bad experience**. Identifying their concerns early allows businesses to fix problems and regain trust.

---

## 4. Passives Are a Hidden Opportunity

Passives sit on the fence. With small improvements to their experience, they can easily become **loyal promoters**.

---

## 5. Promoters Are Free Marketers

Promoters often become **unpaid brand ambassadors**. They naturally share positive experiences through word-of-mouth recommendations.

---

## 6. Timing Matters

Sending NPS surveys immediately after a positive interaction increases response rates and improves feedback quality.

---

## 7. Simpler Surveys Get More Responses

Customers prefer **short and simple surveys**. Long surveys reduce response rates significantly.

---

## 8. NPS Helps Identify Trends

Tracking NPS regularly—weekly or monthly—helps businesses detect **customer experience trends** and adjust strategies quickly.

---

## 9. NPS Works Internally Too

NPS can also measure **employee satisfaction** using a similar method called **Employee Net Promoter Score (eNPS)**.

---

## 10. NPS Alone Isn’t Enough

While powerful, NPS should be used alongside other customer experience metrics like:

- CSAT (Customer Satisfaction Score)  
- CES (Customer Effort Score)

Together they provide a more complete picture of customer loyalty.

---

## 11. External Events Can Affect NPS

Global events, economic changes, or crises can influence customer sentiment and affect NPS scores—even if the company’s service quality remains unchanged.

---

## 12. NPS Is Your Business Heartbeat

Think of NPS as a **heartbeat monitor for your business**. A strong score usually means your customer relationships are healthy.

# How to Run an NPS Survey and Collect Feedback

Running an effective NPS survey requires the right approach and tools.

## 1. Identify Your Audience

Decide whether you want feedback from:

- All customers  
- A specific segment  
- Customers after a specific interaction

---

## 2. Ask the NPS Question

Keep the question simple:

> **“On a scale of 0–10, how likely are you to recommend us to others?”**

Follow it with a qualitative question:

> **“What is the main reason for your score?”**

This builds **trust and long-term loyalty**.
