# What is CSAT?

**Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)** is a simple yet powerful metric used to measure how satisfied customers are with a particular interaction or experience.

A typical CSAT question looks like this:

> **How satisfied were you with your recent experience with us?**

- ☐ Very Dissatisfied  
- ☐ Dissatisfied  
- ☐ Neutral  
- ☐ Satisfied  
- ☐ Very Satisfied  

By collecting this feedback quickly, businesses can **identify issues faster and improve customer experiences more effectively**.

# Example of CSAT Calculation

Let’s understand this with an example.

Suppose you send a customer satisfaction survey to **200 customers**.

- 160 customers rated their experience **4 or 5**
- 40 customers gave ratings **between 1 and 3**

Using the formula:

**CSAT = (160 / 200) × 100**

**CSAT Score = 80%**

This means **8 out of 10 customers are satisfied with their experience**.

# How to Collect Accurate CSAT Data

Collecting accurate feedback is critical to measuring customer satisfaction effectively.

## Ask Immediately After the Interaction

Send surveys **right after the customer interaction**. Immediate feedback ensures responses are fresh and more accurate.

## Keep Surveys Short and Clear

Short surveys improve response rates. Ideally, stick to **one simple question**.

Example:

> *How satisfied were you with your experience?*

## Use Consistent Rating Scales

Use consistent scales such as:

- **1–5 scale**
- **1–10 scale**

Consistency ensures more reliable data across surveys.

## Choose the Right Channels

Use channels where customers are most active:

- Email  
- SMS  
- Website pop-ups  
- Mobile apps  

## Segment Your Audience

Segment feedback based on:

- Customer type  
- Region  
- Product category  

This helps uncover deeper insights.

## Add a Follow-Up Question

After customers give a rating, ask an open-ended question such as:

> **What could we do to improve your experience?**

This provides valuable qualitative insights.
